"La Voleuse des Toits", the self-published novel by Laure Dargelos

"La Voleuse des Toits", is a young adult fantasy novel written by Laure Dargelos self-published in 2019. It is her very first novel. Born in 1991, the author, previously a lawyer, devotes her time to writing.

A blood-curdling reality

The story takes us to a dystopian universe, an imaginary world governed by Omega, a mysterious masked character whose identity is unknown to everyone, and the Scarlet League, an alliance between the 12 most powerful families of the kingdom of Orme.

In this tyrannical regime, where art, literature and music are forbidden, breaking the rules is punishable by death.

A rebellious heroine

Against this repressive society, Éléonore Herreinstein, a young lady from the aristocracy, is ready to do anything to overthrow the government. At night, she becomes Plume: disguised as a boy, she violates the curfew and roams the rooftops, while she risks being caught by the militia. As a sign of protest, the young girl in search of freedom draws huge frescoes on the wall, in order to transmit messages.

But when a young heir of the Scarlet League asks for her hand, her destiny is upset, especially since he has unmasked Eleanor about her nocturnal activities.

A breathtaking writing style

If there is one book that I recommend to myself to reread later, it is this one, which is my favorite novel. The theme of time travel is a subject that interests me a lot. The author's writing is simply beautiful, with a very rich vocabulary, not to mention the plot which was great. The story is incredibly well crafted.

The kingdom lives in manipulation, with an oppressive political regime. Among the population, there are still rebels who refuse to submit to this corrupt system. These opponents fight for the freedom of the people, despite the fatal consequences that threaten them, as great inequalities are created in the Nether and all forms of expression are prohibited.

As this novel was self-published, the author was able to publish her work as she wished, with a certain freedom and without the restrictions of publishing houses. This makes "La Voleuse des Toits" an authentic novel.
