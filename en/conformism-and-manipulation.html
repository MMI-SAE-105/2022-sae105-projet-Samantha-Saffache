Conformism and manipulation

Conformism is present everywhere, in our daily life. It is a kind of social pressure, of mental manipulation, which pushes people to do something according to the "normality".

The Asch effect

A well-known study by Solomon Asch shows how much conformity impacts our lives. The purpose of the study was to see how the latter would react to the behavior of the other participants. The test was given to a group of students, including accomplices and a single naive subject.

The instruction is simple: a model line is given and one must, among the three choices proposed, determine which line is identical, then which is the longest or the shortest. The accomplices all give the wrong answer, and the naive subject always answers second to last.

When the answers are different, the subject answers without any problem, whereas when there is unanimity of wrong answers from the accomplices, the subject tends to conform to the rest of the group, even if it is obvious to him that it is wrong. Some even believe that they have a vision problem.

Fear of others' gaze

Often unconsciously, people feel compelled to do something based on others. If their judgement conflicts with propriety, they step aside to conform to society's expectations. Those who are not confident in themselves are all the more sensitive to this, so they are more likely to conform, not assuming who they really are.

I regret that there are no more "Postman Cheval", Eleonore Herreinstein, or Professor Keating. Because not only do I not want to enter into this conformity, but I see that many people are unhappy. They haven't fulfilled their lives

So I think it's unfortunate that so many people let themselves be shaped by conformity, because so many things change over time. What was frowned upon in the days of the Postman Cheval would be the work of a genius today. Many people are afraid of asserting themselves when, if they realized their dreams, they might be considered artists tomorrow.
